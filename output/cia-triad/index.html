CIA Triad

A foundational model in information security that emphasizes the importance of confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

Description

The CIA Triad is a crucial framework in the field of information security, representing the three primary objectives that organizations must focus on to protect their information assets. Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to those authorized to have access, thereby preventing unauthorized disclosure. Integrity involves maintaining the accuracy and reliability of data, ensuring that information remains unaltered and trustworthy throughout its lifecycle. Availability ensures that information and resources are accessible to authorized users when needed, preventing disruptions in service. In the context of understanding non-human identities, such as digital identities or AI systems, the CIA Triad helps address the security concerns related to data processing and storage by non-human agents. These agents must be designed to uphold these principles to prevent data breaches, ensure data fidelity, and maintain system uptime. As technology evolves, applying the CIA Triad to non-human entities becomes increasingly vital to safeguard both user data and organizational resources.

Examples

  • Implementing encryption to maintain data confidentiality.
  • Using checksums and hashes to verify data integrity.

Additional Information

  • The CIA Triad is fundamental in developing security policies.
  • Each component of the triad is interdependent; compromising one affects the others.
