OT (Operational Technology)

Operational Technology refers to hardware and software that detects or causes changes through direct monitoring and control of physical devices, processes, and events.

Description

Operational Technology (OT) encompasses the systems, devices, and software that manage and control physical processes and operations in various industries, such as manufacturing, energy, and transportation. Unlike traditional Information Technology (IT), which focuses on data management and processing, OT is concerned with the real-time collection and analysis of data from the physical world. This includes the use of sensors, control systems, and automation tools that interact directly with machinery and infrastructure. In the context of understanding non-human identities, OT plays a significant role as it increasingly relies on advanced technologies, including the Internet of Things (IoT) and artificial intelligence, to optimize operations and improve efficiency. These systems often operate autonomously or require minimal human intervention, raising questions about the nature of agency, responsibility, and identity in a world where machines play active roles in decision-making processes.

Examples

  • Industrial control systems (ICS) used in manufacturing to manage production lines.
  • Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems for monitoring and controlling critical infrastructure such as water treatment plants.

Additional Information

  • OT systems are increasingly connected to IT networks, leading to convergence and potential cybersecurity risks.
  • The growth of smart factories and Industry 4.0 initiatives is driving advancements in OT.
