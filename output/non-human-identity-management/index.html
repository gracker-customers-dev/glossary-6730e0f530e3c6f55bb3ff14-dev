Non-Human Identity Management

The process of managing identities associated with non-human entities such as devices, applications, and services.

Description

Non-Human Identity Management (NHIM) refers to the strategies and technologies used to manage identities that are not associated with human users. This includes devices like IoT sensors, software applications, and automated services that require unique identifiers to authenticate and authorize them within a network or system. NHIM is crucial in environments where devices interact autonomously, ensuring secure communication and proper access control. As the Internet of Things (IoT) and automation technologies expand, the need for robust NHIM solutions has increased significantly. These systems must address challenges such as scaling, security, and interoperability. Effective NHIM helps in maintaining an organized inventory of non-human identities, enabling operations such as monitoring, reporting, and compliance with security policies. Moreover, NHIM plays a vital role in ensuring that non-human entities can be reliably identified and managed in a way that supports the overall security posture of the organization.

Examples

  • IoT device authentication in smart homes and cities.
  • API keys and tokens used to manage application identities in web services.

Additional Information

  • NHIM is essential for automated systems to prevent unauthorized access.
  • It helps in compliance with regulations like GDPR, particularly regarding device data management.
